Our Work

Advocacy, Empowerment, and Inclusivity

At LAWSANAID, our work is a testament to our commitment to creating an inclusive and empowering environment for individuals with disabilities pursuing legal education. Through a multifaceted approach, we engage in a range of initiatives designed to break down barriers, foster skill development, challenge stereotypes, and actively contribute to a legal community that celebrates diversity.

Our Programs: Empowering
Through Action

At LAWSANAID, we are committed to empowering individuals with disabilities pursuing legal education through a diverse range of programs. Our initiatives are carefully crafted to address the unique challenges faced by aspiring legal professionals with disabilities and provide comprehensive support at every stage of their academic and professional journey.

LAWSANAID Ambassadorsâ€™ Network (LAN): A network of passionate students across Africa committed to the UN SDGs, and connecting same on how to make life better for students with disabilities.

Mental Health Corner (MHC): A safe place for students in Africa, including those with disabilities to garner support while going through school. We organize monthly and quarterly programs, and sessions with experts to deepen mental health conversations.

LAWSANAID Law School Scholarship Grant: An exclusive scholarship grant to law students with disabilities to ensure they attend law schools in Africa like their counterparts. The grant covers their tuition fee, monthly stipends, access to our material bank, free wig and gown, regular check-in.

Self-DAG Week: An annual week-long event on self Development, Awareness and Growth where we have successfully lawyers, including those with disabilities engage the members of our community on different aspects of life. This project has proven to be highly impactful, given the feedback from participants

Annual Community Essay Competition: We host an annual essay competition to imbibe research and writing skills in our members. This project has in so many ways improved the lives of our community members, and their esteem.
